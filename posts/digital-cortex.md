Digital Cortex was an installation project exploring the relationship between digital media and physical interaction.

The piece sits in the same thread as my earlier visual and audio-visual work: using software as a material, not just a delivery mechanism. It was interested in how data, image, space, and bodily presence can shape the way a viewer perceives a gallery environment.
